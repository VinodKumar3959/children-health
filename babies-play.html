Why play is important

Emotions and play: babies

Baby emotions are often expressed and developed through play. Simple play ideas to help babies explore emotions include music, messy play and puppets.

Imagining, creating and play: babies

From birth to 12 months, babies’ imaginations expand all the time. Play is key to imaginative development. Get play ideas and creative activities for babies.

Movement and play: babies

In the first year, babies learn to lift their heads, roll over, sit, crawl, stand and walk. Our play ideas get your baby moving and develop motor skills.

Talking and play: babies

Babies develop talking skills through play. Play ideas to develop baby talking skills include reading and storytelling, nursery rhymes and everyday chat.

Thinking and play: babies

Your baby is thinking and learning all the time. Play ideas for baby cognitive development include reading, playing with balls, blocks, rattles and more.

Babies: getting play started

Letting your child lead play: activities for children 0-6 years

Child-led play is following your child’s lead and responding to what they say or do. It’s good because your child learns most when they’re engaged in play.

Toys and games for kids – what’s best? Finding the right toys and games is about matching play ideas to your child’s age, developmental stage and interests.

Choosing toys for children

The best toys for kids spark imagination, creativity and problem-solving skills. Open-ended toys are good for this. You can often make these toys at home.

Babies explore all the time. It’s how they learn. A sensory bag is a soft bag full of safe, interesting things for babies to touch, taste, look at and more.

Outdoor play

Outdoor play is a big part of growth, development and wellbeing for children. Outdoor play ideas include tummy time, ball play, nature walks and bike rides.

Babies: learning ideas

Learning in the baby to preschool years

Right from birth, children learn by playing and exploring. They learn best by actively engaging with their environments and trying many different activities.

Early numeracy skills: how to develop them

Children develop numeracy and maths skills from birth. Build early numeracy skills by counting, sorting, looking at shapes, singing number songs and more.

Babies: literacy, reading & stories

Developing literacy

Literacy development is learning about sounds, words and language. Everyday activities like singing, talking, reading and play help children develop literacy.

Literacy activities for children

Want fun literacy activities for your child? Sing nursery rhymes, play ‘I spy’, scribble, draw and write together – and read, read, read! Get more tips.

Reading and storytelling with babies and children

Reading and storytelling with babies and children promotes brain development, teaches children about language and emotions, and strengthens relationships.

Reading with babies from birth

Start reading to babies early – the earlier the better. Read slowly. Point out words and pictures. Babies like books and stories with good rhyme and rhythm.

Reading with babies and toddlers: 12-18 months

Reading and storytelling help babies learn. Read daily with your baby. Try books with rhyme and rhythm. Babies might like board books and textured books.

Babies: media & technology

Screen time: checklist for healthy use

Screen time can be part of a healthy lifestyle for your children. Just balance screen time with other activities and encourage kids to use quality content.

Screen time: babies and toddlers

For children aged 0-2 years, most types of screen time are best saved until they’re older. Connecting with family and friends using video chat can be fun.

How children see TV, YouTube, games and movies

When kids watch TV, YouTube, games and movies, violence, sex and scary images affect them in various ways. Think about your child’s age when choosing media.
