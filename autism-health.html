Autism: sleep

Better sleep for autistic children 3-8 years: eight tips

Bedtime routines, regular bedtimes, healthy sleep associations and environments, and healthy daytime habits can help autistic children settle and sleep better.

Sleep problems and solutions: autistic children

Changes to routines, sleep habits and lifestyle might help with autistic children’s sleep problems. It’s good to get professional help for sleep problems.

Autism: toilet training & hygiene

Toilet training: autistic children

Help toilet training go well for autistic children by teaching in steps, using rewards, visual supports and social stories, and making children comfortable.

Toilet training for autistic children: in pictures

Going to the toilet is a task with many parts. For autistic children, it helps to teach each part step by step. This illustrated guide has tips to help.

Personal hygiene and autistic teenagers

Like all teens, autistic teens need to take extra care with personal hygiene. Visual supports, social stories and video-modelling can help them learn how.

Autism: eating concerns

Fussy eating: autistic children and teenagers

Some autistic children and teens have fussy eating. It can help to introduce new foods in non-threatening ways, offer food regularly, and share family meals.

Overeating: autistic children and teenagers

Some autistic children and teenagers have overeating habits. Keep snack foods out of sight, replace snacks with other activities and aim for regular meals.

Autism: mental health

Depression and low mood: autistic teenagers

Signs of depression in autistic teens include changes in behaviour. Helpful strategies include thought detectives, social skills, lifestyle changes and more.

Anxiety: autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children and teenagers can have a lot of anxiety. You can help your child recognise anxiety triggers and feelings and learn how to manage anxiety.

Autism: child sexual abuse

Child sexual abuse in autistic children and teenagers: recognising, responding and reporting

Child sexual abuse signs in autistic children and teens include emotional or behaviour changes and physical injuries. Tell the police about abuse – call 000.

Child sexual abuse: keeping autistic children and teenagers safe

Protect autistic children and teens from sexual abuse by teaching them about public and private body parts, behaviour and places, and also unsafe situations.

Problematic and harmful sexual behaviour in autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children and teenagers might need help to understand acceptable sexual behaviour. They need professional support for problematic sexual behaviour.
