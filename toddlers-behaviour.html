Understanding toddler behaviour

Temperament: what it is and why it matters

Temperament is the individual way your child responds to the world. Understanding your child’s temperament helps you choose effective parenting strategies.

Self-regulation in children and teenagers

Self-regulation is the ability to manage reactions and behaviour. You can help children learn self-regulation from early childhood and into the teen years.

Self-esteem in children: 1-8 years

Self-esteem is liking yourself. It helps children try new things and face challenges. Loving relationships, feedback and encouragement boosts self-esteem.

Toddlers: behaviour management tips & tools

Behaviour management for children: a positive approach

Behaviour management strategies help children learn how to behave. Positive behaviour management means giving children attention when they behave well.

New skills for children: behaviour management strategy

Skill-building is an important behaviour management strategy for children. Help children learn new skills with instructions, modelling and stepwise guidance.

Requests and instructions: helping children cooperate

It’s great when children cooperate, but this doesn’t just happen. Help your child learn about cooperation by using effective instructions and requests.

Changing the environment: behaviour management tool

Your child’s environment can influence his behaviour. If you change your child’s environment, you can often change your child’s behaviour too. Read how.

Distraction: behaviour management tool

Distraction can work well as a child behaviour management strategy, especially when young children are bored or cranky. Get tips for distracting your child.

Positive attention: behaviour management strategy

What’s the secret to encouraging good behaviour in children? Paying attention and giving plenty of positive attention when children behave well. Read more.

Planning ahead: behaviour management strategy

Some situations can spark challenging behaviour from children. Our planning ahead behaviour management strategy has 6 steps that encourage better behaviour.

Routines: behaviour management strategy

Routines work well as a behaviour management strategy because they help kids cooperate. You can also use a routine to plan for challenging child behaviour.

Activity changes: behaviour management strategies

It can be hard for children to change activities. Behaviour management strategies for the changes include routines, warnings, timing, choices and calmness.

Time-in: helping toddlers calm down

Time-in can help young children calm down and cope with strong emotions. It involves staying close, offering comfort, and reassuring children you understand.

Toddlers: crying & tantrums

Crying: children 1-8 years

Children cry when they’re hungry, tired, uncomfortable, in pain, frustrated, angry or upset. When you know why they’re crying, you can work out how to help.

Tantrums: why they happen and how to respond

Toddlers have tantrums because they’re overwhelmed by big emotions. Older children might not yet have learned safe ways to manage feelings. Get tantrum tips. Article available in: Arabic, Dari, Karen, Persian, Simplified Chinese, Vietnamese.

Toddlers: common behaviour concerns

Overstimulation: babies and children

Children can get overstimulated when there’s too much going on. You can help with overstimulation by reducing noise and activity or offering a quiet activity.

Fear of strangers: babies and young children

Fear of strangers is common in babies and young children. Try being patient, introducing new people gradually and building confidence in social situations.

Shyness: babies and children

Some babies and children are naturally slow to warm up or uncomfortable in social situations. If your child experiences shyness, you can help. Get tips.

Fear of the bath: babies and toddlers

Some young children are afraid of the bath. If your child has a fear of baths, introduce baths slowly, help your child feel safe, and make bath time fun.

Biting, pinching and hair-pulling

Biting, pinching and hair-pulling are part of development in babies and toddlers. Saying no, staying calm and withdrawing attention can help. Get more tips.

Breath-holding

Watching your child have a breath-holding spell can be scary. Stay calm and wait, because your child will start breathing again, usually in under a minute.

Lies: why children lie and what to do

Most children lie sometimes. Encourage children to tell the truth by valuing honesty. Use separate consequences for lies and behaviour that leads to lies.

Pestering: what to do about it

Pestering – it can be really frustrating. So how can you handle pester power? This article has practical tips on what to do when children pester.

When children ask for things: how to respond constructively

Children often ask for things. Sometimes you can say yes, but sometimes it’s no. Your responses teach kids about communication, respect and compromise.

Children’s habits

Many children have habits like nail-biting or thumb-sucking, which usually go away by themselves. Gentle reminders and praise can help with breaking habits.

Toddlers: friends & siblings

Toddlers making friends

To make friends, toddlers need to learn friendship skills. Play with you and playdates with other children can help. Try sandpit play, painting and blocks.

Imaginary friends

Imaginary friends come from healthy imaginations. They help children express feelings and practise skills. Children usually grow out of imaginary friends.

Sharing and learning to share

Learning to share can be a challenge for children, but it’s key to getting along with others. Practice, encouragement and sharing games can help kids learn.

Preventing sibling fights: eight tips

The key to fewer sibling fights? Help your children learn to get along. You can do this by using family rules, routines and praise for good behaviour.

Handling sibling fights

What’s the best way to handle sibling fights? Step in when needed, keep calm, make a time to talk about the issue, and apply consequences fairly. Read more.

Toddlers: discipline

Discipline and guiding behaviour: babies and children

Discipline is about helping children learn how to behave. Rules and consequences are key aspects of discipline, as is a loving relationship with your child.

Smacking: what you need to know

Smacking isn’t good for children’s wellbeing or behaviour. It’s better to help your child behave well, and use consequences for children over 3 years.
