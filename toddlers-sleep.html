Toddlers: night-time problems

Body-rocking, head-rolling and head-banging at bedtime

It can be upsetting to see your child body-rocking, head-rolling or head-banging in bed. It’s common, and children often do it as part of setting for sleep.

Teeth-grinding

Children often grind their teeth in sleep. Teeth-grinding doesn’t usually damage teeth, but see a dentist or GP if you’re worried. It’s also called bruxism.

Dummies: helping your child let go

Giving up the dummy can be hard. Make it easier by taking a gradual approach, limiting dummy use, and celebrating when children finally let go of the dummy.

Calling out and getting out of bed

Getting out of bed and calling out are common sleep issues in children. Read how a bedtime routine plus persistence can reduce bedtime problems.

Night terrors

Night terrors can be scary to see, but they don’t hurt children. Don’t wake your child during a night terror. Let it finish, then help your child settle.
