Understanding newborn behaviour

Newborn behaviour: an overview

Cuddling, sleeping, feeding and crying – that’s what newborn behaviour is all about. It’s important to respond quickly and lovingly to newborn behaviour.

Temperament: what it is and why it matters

Temperament is the individual way your child responds to the world. Understanding your child’s temperament helps you choose effective parenting strategies.

Your newborn’s first week: what to expect

A newborn’s first week of life is spent adapting to the outside world. You can help by providing warmth, love, security and cuddles. Here’s what to expect.

Newborns: crying & colic

Crying: babies

All babies cry. If your baby is crying, check that baby isn’t sick, hurt or uncomfortable. Singing, rocking, wrapping, patting, massage or baths might help.

Cry Baby program

The online Cry Baby learning program helps you understand and cope with young baby sleep and crying habits using tried and tested strategies.

Soothing a crying baby: in pictures

To soothe a crying baby, check whether baby is hungry, tired, uncomfortable or sick. This helps you know what to do to comfort baby and stop the crying.

Baby cues and baby body language: a guide

Baby body language gives cues to whether babies are tired or hungry, want to play or need a break. Our guide has videos so you can see baby cues in action.

Colic: what it is and what to do

Colic is when babies cry and fuss a lot for no obvious medical reason. It’s best to see a GP or nurse about colic. Looking after yourself is important too.

Newborns: common behaviour concerns

Overstimulation: babies and children

Children can get overstimulated when there’s too much going on. You can help with overstimulation by reducing noise and activity or offering a quiet activity.

Fear of the bath: babies and toddlers

Some young children are afraid of the bath. If your child has a fear of baths, introduce baths slowly, help your child feel safe, and make bath time fun.
