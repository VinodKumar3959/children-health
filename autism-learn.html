Learning about autism

About autism

Autism: what is it?

Autistic children have communication difficulties, narrow interests, repetitive behaviour and sometimes sensory issues. Autism is a brain-based condition.

Causes of autism

There’s no single, known cause of autism. Causes of autism might include genes, brain development, family history and other factors.

Learning and development in autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children develop skills at different rates and in a different order from other children. Focusing on strengths can help autistic children learn.

Thinking and learning strengths in autistic children and pre-teens

Autistic children often have strengths in visual, rule-based and interest-based thinking. You can build children’s skills by working with these strengths.

Conditions that can occur with autism

Autistic children can have other conditions, which are called co-occurring conditions. This A-Z guide outlines the 14 most commonly co-occurring conditions.

Vaccinations and autism

There’s no scientific evidence to support a link between autism and childhood vaccinations. Research proposing the link has been discredited and retracted.

Autism language on raisingchildren.net.au

raisingchildren.net.au uses identity-first language to talk about autism. This approach recognises that autism is an inherent part of a person’s identity.

Autism: assessment & diagnosis

Worried your child is autistic: what to do

If you’re worried your child is autistic, see an autism professional, get early intervention, get an assessment, build support and gather information.

Early signs of autism

Early signs of autism often appear at 1-2 years. They include unusual social and communication behaviour, repetitive movements and restricted interests.

Signs of autism in older children and teenagers

Signs of autism in teenagers and older children sometimes become noticeable at school, when children have difficulties with new social situations and skills.

Autism diagnosis for children: a guide

How is autism diagnosed in children? Autism diagnosis usually involves many specialists and tests to help with assessing a child’s strengths and challenges.

Late autism diagnosis: older children and teenagers

Sometimes children or teens get a late autism diagnosis because autism signs become obvious only in adolescence. Diagnosis helps teens get the right support.

DSM-5: autism spectrum disorder diagnosis

The Diagnostic and statistical manual of mental disorders, or DSM-5, lists autism signs and says how many signs must be present for an autism diagnosis.
