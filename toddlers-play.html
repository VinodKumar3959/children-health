Toddlers: play & development

Why play is important

Emotions and play: toddlers

Empathy, pride, frustration are common toddler emotions. Play ideas to develop toddler emotions include singing, messy play, puppet play and outdoor play.

Imagining, creating and play: toddlers

Toddlers explore the world and expand their imaginations through play. Imaginative and creative play helps them develop many different skills. Get play ideas.

Movement and play: toddlers

Play is important for toddler motor skills development. Play ideas for toddler movement include music play, ride-on-toys and trikes, outdoor play and more.

Talking and play: toddlers

Toddlers develop talking skills through play. Play ideas to help toddlers with talking include chatting together, singing, storytelling and naming games.

Play ideas for toddler talking and reading: in pictures

Toddlers develop through play. Get illustrated play ideas to develop toddler talking and reading, including singing, word and naming games, and stories.

Thinking and play: toddlers

Your toddler is starting to learn and understand many new concepts. Play ideas for toddler cognitive development include reading, sorting, puzzles and craft.

Toddlers: getting play started

Letting your child lead play: activities for children 0-6 years

Child-led play is following your child’s lead and responding to what they say or do. It’s good because your child learns most when they’re engaged in play.

Toddlers at play

Toddlers want to learn about the world – they do this mainly through play. Try toddler play ideas and toddler games like messy play, dress-ups and reading.

Toys and games for children

Toys and games for kids – what’s best? Finding the right toys and games is about matching play ideas to your child’s age, developmental stage and interests.

Choosing toys for children

The best toys for kids spark imagination, creativity and problem-solving skills. Open-ended toys are good for this. You can often make these toys at home.

Toddlers: low-cost play ideas

Collage: activity for children 2-6 years

Collage is a simple craft activity. Children paste materials onto paper. Collage is great for fine motor skills and colour and texture awareness.

Homemade toys and free activities for children

A little imagination can turn ordinary items into homemade toys and free activities for kids. Get ideas for babies, toddlers, preschoolers and school kids.

Cubbyhouse activity: children 2-6 years

Cubbyhouses are great for imaginative play, creative thinking and problem-solving. You can make a cubbyhouse with things around your home. Here are ideas.

Guess the animal game: activity for children 1-6 years

Our guess the animal game gets children using imagination and thinking skills. It can be good exercise too. You and your child can play this game anywhere.

Dress-up games: activity for children 3-6 years

Dress-up games let your child act out different roles, explore ideas, develop imagination, and express emotions in a safe way. Here’s how to get started.

Playing with cardboard boxes: activities for children 2-6 years

Cardboard boxes are great for imaginative play. Your child can use them to make castles, shops, caves and more. Cardboard boxes also encourage physical play.

Peg play: activity for children 2-4 years

Through peg play children can develop numeracy and motor skills and enjoy experimental play. All you need is plastic pegs and some other household items.

Toddlers: outdoor play

Outdoor play

Outdoor play is a big part of growth, development and wellbeing for children. Outdoor play ideas include tummy time, ball play, nature walks and bike rides.

Toddlers: active play

Physical activity for young children

Daily physical activity is good for babies, toddlers and preschoolers. When you make physical activity fun and part of family life, it’s easy to get moving.

Rough-and-tumble play: a guide

Rough play and play fighting can be fun. It can also help children develop social and physical skills. Ground rules can prevent children from getting hurt.

Rough-and-tumble play: activities for children 3 months-8 years

Rough-and-tumble play is climbing, wrestling, rolling around and play fighting. It’s fun and good exercise. Get ideas for rough-and-tumble play activities.

Sharing and learning to share

Learning to share can be a challenge for children, but it’s key to getting along with others. Practice, encouragement and sharing games can help kids learn.

Toddlers: learning ideas

Learning in the baby to preschool years

Right from birth, children learn by playing and exploring. They learn best by actively engaging with their environments and trying many different activities.

Early numeracy skills: how to develop them

Children develop numeracy and maths skills from birth. Build early numeracy skills by counting, sorting, looking at shapes, singing number songs and more.

Handwriting skills for children

For toddlers and preschoolers, handwriting starts with drawing with crayons and chalk. Older children learn formal handwriting at school. Read how to help.

Toddlers: literacy, reading & stories

Developing literacy

Literacy development is learning about sounds, words and language. Everyday activities like singing, talking, reading and play help children develop literacy.

Literacy activities for children

Want fun literacy activities for your child? Sing nursery rhymes, play ‘I spy’, scribble, draw and write together – and read, read, read! Get more tips.

Reading activities: children 2-4 years

Reading helps child development in many ways. Start with picture books that have rhyme and rhythm. Try to share at least one book with your child each day.

Looking for words: literacy activity for children 1-5 years

It’s easy to help children learn literacy skills. For example, looking for words while you’re shopping is a fun way to promote literacy development.

Library activities: children 3-6 years

Reading and storytelling with babies and children

Reading and storytelling with babies and children promotes brain development, teaches children about language and emotions, and strengthens relationships.

Reading with babies and toddlers: 12-18 months

Reading and storytelling help babies learn. Read daily with your baby. Try books with rhyme and rhythm. Babies might like board books and textured books.

Reading with toddlers: 18 months-3 years

Reading and storytelling helps toddlers learn. Try to read daily. Toddlers like animal books, stories about playtime, and lift-the-flap and pop-up books.

Toddlers: gifted & talented children

About gifted and talented children and teenagers

Gifted children are born with advanced abilities. Talented children have developed these abilities to a high level. Read about gifted and talented children.

Identification of gifted and talented children

If your child has advanced natural abilities, they might be identified as gifted and talented. Giftedness can be identified either formally or informally.

Behaviour, emotions, social development: gifted and talented children

Gifted and talented children can have strong feelings and challenging behaviour. Communication, calming down, social skills and self-compassion can help.

Gifted and talented children: talking about their abilities

Talking about your gifted and talented child’s abilities can help you make good decisions that support your child’s development, but it can be tricky.

Supporting learning for gifted and talented children

Everyday play and activities can help gifted and talented children develop. Structured learning activities can also extend learning and develop talents.

Programs for gifted and talented children: school and community

Programs for gifted and talented children take many forms, but they should all offer stimulating experiences. Get contacts for gifted and talented programs.

Gifted and talented children: child care to school

The key to a successful experience for your gifted and talented child at child care, preschool and school is good communication with teachers. Get tips.

Family life with gifted and talented children

Family routines and rules are good for relationships in families with gifted and talented children. They help all children feel loved, valued and accepted.

Toddlers: screen time & media choices

Screen time: checklist for healthy use

Screen time can be part of a healthy lifestyle for your children. Just balance screen time with other activities and encourage kids to use quality content.

Screen time: babies and toddlers

For children aged 0-2 years, most types of screen time are best saved until they’re older. Connecting with family and friends using video chat can be fun.

Preschooler screen time: tips for balance

Screen time can be part of a healthy lifestyle for preschoolers when balanced with physical play, creative play, reading, socialising and other activities.

Healthy posture for children’s screen time: in pictures

When children are enjoying screen time, healthy posture helps them avoid pain, strain and discomfort. Encourage children to change position and move around.

How children see TV, YouTube, games and movies

When kids watch TV, YouTube, games and movies, violence, sex and scary images affect them in various ways. Think about your child’s age when choosing media.

Disaster news and distressing news events: supporting children 2-5 years

Media coverage of natural disasters, terrorism, crime, accidents and the pandemic can upset young children. Talking is the best way to help your child cope.
