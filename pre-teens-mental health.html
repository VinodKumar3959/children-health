Pre-teens: about mental health

Mental health in pre-teens and teenagers

Teen mental health is about happiness, good relationships, exercise, belonging and more. Signs of mental health problems include mood and other changes.

Happiness and wellbeing for pre-teens and teenagers

Happy teenagers have warm relationships, try new things, have goals and focus on positive things in life. Get tips to boost teenage wellbeing and happiness.

Moods: helping pre-teens and teens manage emotional ups and downs

Moods, or emotional ups and downs, are a normal and important part of teen development. Help teens manage moods by listening, talking and staying connected.

Self-compassion for pre-teens and teenagers

Self-compassion is treating yourself kindly when things don’t go well. When teens learn and practise self-compassion, they feel more confident and resilient.

Pre-teens: practical mental health & wellbeing strategies

Mindfulness

Mindfulness is paying attention to the present moment. It can help with stress, anxiety and depression. You can do it with your children too. Here’s how.

Mindfulness activity for children, teenagers and parents

Mindfulness is focusing on what’s happening right now. Parents and children can learn and practise it together with our mindfulness of sounds activity.

Positive thinking: activity for children, teenagers and parents

Want to feel happier? Try this positive thinking activity with your children. It gets you all thinking positive thoughts about good things and how you’ve made them happen.

Grounding: a calming exercise for children and teenagers

Grounding exercises help children calm down after distressing experiences. In a grounding exercise, children breathe deeply and focus on things around them.

Breathing exercises: relaxation activity for children, teenagers and parents

Breathing exercises help you breathe deeply and feel calm, especially in stressful situations. You and your child can do breathing exercises together.

Muscle relaxation activity for children, teenagers and parents

Muscle relaxation helps you and your child feel calm and physically relaxed. To do muscle relaxation, tense and relax each part of your body in turn.

Pre-teens: mental health disorders

Obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) in children and teenagers

Obsessive compulsive disorder (OCD) is an anxiety disorder. It can stop children from enjoying life. If you’re worried about your child, talk with your GP.

Oppositional defiant disorder (ODD): children 5-12 years

Oppositional defiant disorder (ODD) is more than bad behaviour. Children with ODD can’t cooperate and need professional help to manage their behaviour.

Eating disorders in older children and teenagers

Eating disorders are serious illnesses. Signs include changes in eating, behaviour and physical health. Children and teens with eating disorders need help.

Pre-teens: stress, anxiety & depression

Stress and stress management: pre-teens and teenagers

Stress is a response to external pressures. Some stress is OK. Too much is overwhelming. Help teens manage stress by talking and encouraging healthy choices.

Depression: pre-teens and teenagers

Pre-teen and teenage depression is serious. Warning signs include changes in your child’s thoughts, emotions, behaviour and body. Start by seeing your GP.

Anxiety and how to manage it: pre-teens and teenagers

Most teenagers feel anxious sometimes. You can help teens manage anxiety by talking and listening, spending time together and encouraging healthy choices.

Anxiety disorders: pre-teens and teenagers

An anxiety disorder is when everyday anxiety becomes severe and long-lasting. Teenagers with anxiety disorders usually recover well with professional help.

Selective mutism in children and teenagers

Children with selective mutism can’t speak in certain situations. This can interfere with their daily life and development. Children need professional help.

Pre-teens: mental health therapies & services

Choosing mental health services for pre-teens and teenagers

The best mental health services for teenagers are the ones that meet your child’s needs. It’s also important to ask about treatments and check accreditation.

Mental health services for pre-teens and teenagers

The teenage mental health services system can be a maze. Our guide helps you and your teenage child understand the system and get the best service.

Mental health treatments and therapies for pre-teens and teenagers

Teens and pre-teens with mental health problems have many treatment options, including counselling, psychotherapy, CBT, e-therapy, family therapy and more.

Mental health assessment: pre-teens and teenagers

Worried about your teenager’s mental health? A mental health assessment can identify your child’s issues and form the basis of a treatment plan.

Mental health professionals for pre-teens and teenagers: what to expect

Expect mental health professionals for teens to be competent, skilled, knowledgeable and caring. These qualities underpin good professional relationships.

Mental health professionals: a guide for pre-teens and teenagers

Mental health professionals for teenagers include GPs, psychologists, psychiatrists, counsellors, occupational therapists and social workers. Find out more.

Pre-teens: health care

Teenage health care: rights and responsibilities

As teens get older, they can start using the health care system on their own. They have rights to consent and confidentiality, as well as responsibilities.

Finding a family GP

A good family GP understands the importance of getting to know you and your children. Location, opening hours, fees and availability are important too.

Teenagers seeing the GP alone

Teenagers need to start seeing the family GP by themselves, so they can develop health independence. You can help your child make this shift gradually.

Telehealth for children and teenagers

Telehealth is when you have health appointments by phone or video. Telehealth has many benefits for families. It’s secure and protects privacy.

Telehealth for children and teenagers: getting the most out of appointments

Telehealth appointments work well when you’re prepared. Check technology. Make sure documents, photos and videos are ready. Tell children what to expect.

Telehealth appointments for children and teenagers: in pictures

Our guide shows you how to get the most out of telehealth appointments: set up somewhere quiet, check technology, prepare children and have information handy.

Medicines for children: a guide

Children’s medicines are designed for children’s bodies. Ask your doctor or pharmacist about which medicines your child needs, and check dosage instructions.

Being an advocate for your child

Being an advocate means speaking for your child. If your child’s needs aren’t being met or they’re being hurt or excluded, you might need to be an advocate.

Pre-teens: health concerns

Childhood obesity

Childhood obesity is when children have too much body fat. A family lifestyle with enough healthy food and physical activity helps children avoid obesity.

Overweight and obesity management in teenagers

Unhealthy weight gain can be a risk for teenagers. Help your child manage weight with a healthy lifestyle, and get help for overweight and obesity issues.

Second-hand smoke and third-hand smoke: effects on children

Second-hand smoke and third-hand smoke can cause serious health problems for children. To reduce the risk, quit smoking and have a smoke-free home and car.

Taking your child’s temperature

Digital probe thermometers are the most accurate way to check your child’s temperature. You can also use digital ear or forehead thermometers. Find out more.

Fever

If your child’s temperature is higher than 38°C, it’s probably a fever. A fever is a sign of illness. Here’s what to do when your child has a fever.

COVID-19 and children in Australia

For most children, COVID-19 causes mild flu-like symptoms. Children should do a COVID test if they have symptoms. You can manage mild COVID symptoms at home.

Colds

A cold can make your child feel miserable. There’s no cure, but you can treat cold symptoms so your child feels more comfortable. Fluids are important.

Circumcision

Experts agree that the risks of circumcision for boys for non-medical reasons outweigh benefits. It’s important to make an informed choice about circumcision.

Swimming pool hygiene

Sometimes germs in swimming pools cause gastro and ear, eye, skin and chest infections. Swimming pool hygiene helps your family stay healthy in pool water.

Pre-teens: allergies & intolerances

Allergies in children and teenagers

Allergies in children – what are they? And what can you do about them? This guide explains causes and treatments for allergies in children and teenagers.

Non-food allergies in children and teenagers

Children should avoid non-food items that give them allergies, like chemicals, dust mites, grasses, weeds or trees, insect bites, latex, medicines and pets.

Food allergies in children and teenagers

If your child has food allergies, your child must avoid the foods that they’re allergic to. You need to know the symptoms and how to treat an allergic reaction.

Food intolerances in children and teenagers

Food intolerance symptoms in children and teens include bloating, diarrhoea and stomach pain. If you think your child has food intolerance, talk to your GP.

Lactose intolerance: babies, children and teenagers

Lactose intolerance symptoms include wind, stomach pain, bloating and diarrhoea. Babies might also have nappy rash. Your child should see the GP.

Pre-teens: chronic conditions

Teenagers with chronic conditions: independence

You can help teenagers with chronic conditions learn to manage their conditions, develop health independence and figure what they want to do as young adults.

Anxiety: children with disability and chronic conditions 5-11 years

Children with disability or chronic conditions are more likely to have anxiety than other children. Appropriate information, choices and planning might help.

Depression: children with disability and chronic conditions 5-11 years

Childhood depression is a serious mental health issue. Children with disability are at higher risk of depression than peers. They need professional support.

Starting secondary school: children with disability

Children with disability starting secondary school might need extra support, including a transition plan, student support group and individual learning plan.

Choosing secondary schools for children with disability

For children with disability, start thinking about secondary schools in Year 5. Visit secondary schools to find out how they support and include children.

Pre-teens: child sexual abuse

Child sexual abuse: what it is and what to do

Child sexual abuse is when adults or older children involve children in sexual activity. Sexual abuse is never a child’s fault. Read how to protect children.

Child sexual abuse: talking to children 0-11 years

Talking about child sexual abuse helps protect children aged 0-11 years. You can talk about feeling unsafe, inappropriate touch, secrets and saying ‘no’.

Preventing child sexual abuse in your family and community

Preventing child sexual abuse starts with your family and community. Prevent abuse by developing family safety rules and working with local organisations.

Signs of sexual abuse in children and teenagers

Children and teenagers might not tell you about sexual abuse. Instead you might notice signs of sexual abuse, like physical, emotional or behaviour changes.

Signs that someone is involved in child sexual abuse

People who sexually abuse children are likely to be people you know. Signs include buying a child expensive gifts or trying to spend time alone with a child.

Grooming: recognising the signs

Grooming is when a person gets friendly with a child or family for later sexual abuse. Signs include a person taking special interest in a family or child.

Child sexual abuse: what to do if a child is sexually abused

If a child tells you about sexual abuse, stay calm, let the child talk, believe the child and help the child feel safe. Report abuse – call police on 000.

Child sexual abuse: help and support for children

Children who have experienced sexual abuse can heal with professional support. Love, reassurance, routines, family time, and talking and listening also help.

Child sexual abuse: helplines and services

If children or teens have experienced child sexual abuse, call police on 000. You can also call state or territory child protection services. Get contacts.

Problematic and harmful sexual behaviour in children and teenagers

Most sexual behaviour in childhood and adolescence is typical and healthy. But some sexual behaviour is problematic or harmful. It needs professional advice.

Supporting children who have engaged in harmful sexual behaviour with other children

Harmful sexual behaviour in children can take many forms. Professional support is essential to helping children who have engaged in harmful sexual behaviour.

Circle of friends: personal boundaries activity for children 3-15 years

A circle of friends activity shows different people in a child’s life. It helps children learn about personal boundaries and stay safe from sexual abuse.
