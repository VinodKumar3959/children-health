Becoming a grandparent

Becoming a grandparent can change your life. It might take you a little while to get used to your new role. What kind of grandparent do you want to be?

Grandparents and family relationships

As a grandparent, you have a big role in supporting your children as they raise grandchildren. This support can be good for all your family relationships.

Grandparents: roles and boundaries

There are many ways to be a grandparent. You might like being hands on or prefer to have less involvement. It’s good to be clear about what role suits you.

Grandparents: when your grandchild’s parents divorce

If your grandchild’s parents separate or divorce, you might be worried about the whole family. Any support you can give will help them adjust to the change.

Grandparents: looking after grandchildren

If you’re a grandparent, you might be asked to look after your grandchildren sometimes. The key is being clear about how much child care you’re able to do.

Grandparents and kinship carers: getting started and finding support

Being a kinship carer

Kinship care is when children can’t live with parents, so relatives or friends become primary carers. Kinship carers need support in this important role.
