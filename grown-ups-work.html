Grown-ups: starting & settling children into child care

Starting child care: children with disability, autism or other additional needs

An individual education plan, visits, meetings with educators and professional support can help children with disability make a positive start at child care.

Starting child care: children’s feelings

Many children have separation anxiety and fear of strangers when starting child care. It’s a typical part of development. It helps to take things slowly.

When your child is settling in at child care, try to ease into the new routine. Stay calm at drop-off. Give your child rest, play and attention at home.

Some children need help to settle into child care. Sharing information and building relationships with educators is important. A goodbye routine can help too.
