Teens: learning

Learning: primary and secondary school years

In the primary and high school years, children and teens learn by observing, questioning, exploring and experimenting – inside and outside school.

Teens: learning difficulties

Learning difficulties and learning disorders: children and teenagers

Learning difficulties are reading, writing and/or maths problems. Learning disorders are serious, ongoing and diagnosed literacy and numeracy problems.

Dyslexia

Dyslexia is a learning disorder. Children with dyslexia have trouble with reading and spelling. Support helps children with dyslexia achieve and succeed.

Teens: choosing, starting & moving school

Secondary schools: things to consider when choosing a school

If you’re looking beyond the local secondary school, you can consider how schools will suit your child’s strengths and needs, as well as your family values.

Primary and secondary schools: practical guide to finding a school and enrolling

Our guide to finding schools takes you through key steps: check enrolment timelines, gather information, visit schools, talk to staff, and apply or enrol.

Starting secondary school

Starting secondary school means big changes. Routines, healthy food, sleep, opportunities to talk, and support for new friendships can ease the transition.

Moving schools

Moving primary or high schools is common. Make moving schools easier by getting children involved in decisions and being organised before the first day.

Teens: school & homework tips

Homework

Homework can be useful, especially for older kids and teens. Help kids by making time and space for homework, helping them get organised and being positive.

Talking about school

‘How was school today?’ is a big question. To get kids talking about school, try asking simple, positive questions. It shows you care about their learning.

Home learning for teenagers during COVID-19 isolation, quarantine or lockdown: tips

To help teens with home learning in isolation, use routines and help them set up a good workspace. Tune in to teens’ feelings and stay in touch with school.

Wearing face masks: tips to help children and teenagers

In some states and territories, children and teens are required to wear face masks when out of their homes or at school. Use our tips to help kids adapt.

Returning to school after COVID-19 isolation, quarantine or lockdown: teenagers

Returning to school after COVID-19 isolation or lockdown is important for teenage wellbeing. Active listening can help teens who are anxious or reluctant.

Teens: working with schools & teachers

Schools, teachers and parents: building strong relationships

Building and maintaining a good parent-school relationship is a great way to support your child’s education. And it’s never too late to start. Here’s how.

Parent-teacher interviews: secondary school

At secondary school parent-teacher interviews, you can check how your child is going in different subjects, and work out how best to support their learning.

Being an advocate for your child

Being an advocate means speaking for your child. If your child’s needs aren’t being met or they’re being hurt or excluded, you might need to be an advocate.

Teens: truancy & other school problems

Truancy: pre-teens and teenagers

It’s important to address truancy problems early. If you know your child is wagging school, talk with your child first and also seek help from the school.

School refusal: children and teenagers

School refusal is when children and teens get upset about going to school, and won’t or can’t go. It’s best to work with schools to get children going again.

School problems: pre-teens and teenagers

School problems are common in the teen years and include lack of engagement and confidence, poor results and so on. It’s important to address problems early.

Teens: bullying & school

Teenage bullying: what to look for and how to help

Teenage bullying is never OK. If you’re worried your child is being bullied, look for signs of problems at school, as well as emotional and physical signs.

Teenagers bullying others: what to do

If your teen is bullying others, you need to acknowledge the behaviour. Then you need to work with the school on changing your child’s bullying behaviour.

Cyberbullying: what it is and how to avoid it

Cyberbullying is using digital technology to hurt others. It’s never OK. Help your child avoid cyberbullying with technology rules and open communication.

Cyberbullying: spotting the signs and helping children and teenagers handle it

Young people experiencing cyberbullying need help. Cyberbullying signs include mood and behaviour changes. Use the G.E.T.R.I.D. steps to handle cyberbullying.

How to stop cyberbullying: in pictures

Download or print this illustrated guide on how to stop cyberbullying. Get tips for dealing effectively with cyberbullies using the G.E.T.R.I.D. steps.

Teens: gifted & talented children

About gifted and talented children and teenagers

Gifted children are born with advanced abilities. Talented children have developed these abilities to a high level. Read about gifted and talented children.

Identification of gifted and talented children

If your child has advanced natural abilities, they might be identified as gifted and talented. Giftedness can be identified either formally or informally.

Behaviour, emotions, social development: gifted and talented children

Gifted and talented children can have strong feelings and challenging behaviour. Communication, calming down, social skills and self-compassion can help.

Gifted and talented children: talking about their abilities

Talking about your gifted and talented child’s abilities can help you make good decisions that support your child’s development, but it can be tricky.

Supporting learning for gifted and talented children

Everyday play and activities can help gifted and talented children develop. Structured learning activities can also extend learning and develop talents.

Programs for gifted and talented children: school and community

Programs for gifted and talented children take many forms, but they should all offer stimulating experiences. Get contacts for gifted and talented programs.

Gifted and talented children: child care to school

The key to a successful experience for your gifted and talented child at child care, preschool and school is good communication with teachers. Get tips.

Family life with gifted and talented children

Family routines and rules are good for relationships in families with gifted and talented children. They help all children feel loved, valued and accepted.

Teens: casual work, careers & further study

Casual work and part-time jobs for teenagers

Casual work and part-time jobs are good for teen confidence and skills. You might need to help teens look for work, prepare résumés and practise interviews.

Jobs, careers, further study and training: getting ready

If your teenager is thinking about careers, first jobs, university, TAFE study or a gap year, our guide has essential tips on career and study pathways.
