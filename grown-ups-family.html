It takes time to build relationships in your blended family. It helps to understand different relationships in blended families and how to nurture them.

The first two years in your blended family

Settling into a blended family takes time. Preparing children and thinking ahead about things like family routines, chores and money management can help.

Family rules and boundaries can be tricky in a blended family. Give children time to get used to things, and try to work together with your partner on rules.

Some children feel excited about living in a blended family; others feel negative. If you try to understand these feelings, it can help your child adjust.

Grown-ups: parents like me

Becoming parents with IVF

IVF is a medical process that might help you have a baby if you can’t conceive naturally. The IVF experience can have many ups and downs.

Parenting with a mental illness

If you have a mental illness, it might be hard to be the parent you want to be. But you’re not alone – help is available. Start by talking with your GP.

Parenting with an intellectual disability

Having an intellectual disability doesn’t mean you can’t be a good parent. It just means that you might need extra support to raise your children.

Raising adopted children

When you adopt, you give a child a home and a sense of belonging, security and identity. It’s good to tell children about their adoption as early as you can.

Raising foster children

Foster care is out-of-home care for children who can’t live with their own families. Being a foster parent can be challenging but also very rewarding.

Parenting with a physical disability

If you’re parenting with a physical disability, you and your children are probably very good at finding creative ways to overcome everyday challenges.

Parenting and problematic alcohol and other drug use

If your alcohol or other drug use has become problematic, it might be hard to care for your children and give them what they need. You can get help to change.

Parenting as a teenager

Teenage parents face similar ups and downs to older parents. But parenting as a teenager does have special challenges, including finishing your education.
