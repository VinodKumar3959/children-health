Grown-ups: anxiety & depression before & after birth

Antenatal depression and postnatal depression: pregnant women and birthing mothers

If pregnant women and birthing mothers experience negative emotional changes that last longer than 2 weeks, it might be antenatal or postnatal depression.

Antenatal and postnatal depression in your partner: how to help

If your partner has antenatal or postnatal depression, your support makes a big difference. It’s also important to get professional support for your partner.

Antenatal and postnatal depression: fathers and all non-birthing parents

Fathers and all non-birthing parents can get antenatal and postnatal depression. Seek help for emotional and other changes that last longer than 2 weeks.

Antenatal anxiety and postnatal anxiety

If you’re having symptoms of antenatal or postnatal (perinatal) anxiety that are hard to control and interfere with daily life, it’s important to get help.

Grown-ups: communication & conflict with partners

Grown-ups: anger, anxiety & stress

Anger and anger management for parents

Anger is a natural human emotion. Managing your anger positively is good for children. Anger management techniques and health professionals can help you.

Anxiety and how to deal with it: tips for parents

It’s important to deal with anxiety if it interferes with health, daily life or relationships. Relaxation techniques can help, as can professional support.

Social anxiety in parents: how to manage it

It’s good for parents to manage social anxiety. Try preparing for and gradually facing social situations, using positive self-talk and working on wellbeing.

Feeling stressed? It’s pretty natural when you’re a parent. Some stress can be good. But if you’re overwhelmed, our stress management techniques can help.

Grown-ups: wellbeing

Self-compassion for parents

Self-compassion is being kind to yourself and reminding yourself that you’re doing your best even if things don’t go well. It’s good for you and your child.

Mindfulness

Mindfulness is paying attention to the present moment. It can help with stress, anxiety and depression. You can do it with your children too. Here’s how.

Mindfulness activity for children, teenagers and parents

Mindfulness is focusing on what’s happening right now. Parents and children can learn and practise it together with our mindfulness of sounds activity.

Positive thinking: activity for children, teenagers and parents

Want to feel happier? Try this positive thinking activity with your children. It gets you all thinking positive thoughts about good things and how you’ve made them happen.

Breathing exercises: relaxation activity for children, teenagers and parents

Breathing exercises help you breathe deeply and feel calm, especially in stressful situations. You and your child can do breathing exercises together.

Muscle relaxation activity for children, teenagers and parents

Muscle relaxation helps you and your child feel calm and physically relaxed. To do muscle relaxation, tense and relax each part of your body in turn.

Trauma: coping and looking after yourself

If you and your child have been through a traumatic event, looking after yourself will help you cope. And if you’re coping, your child will cope better too.

Death of a partner or child’s other parent: coping with your own grief

If your partner or child’s other parent dies, looking after yourself can help you cope with grief. If you’re coping, your child will cope better too.
