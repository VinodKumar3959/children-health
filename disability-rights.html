Children with disability: rights & laws

Children with disability: rights

Inclusion: children and teenagers with disability, developmental delay or autism

Inclusion is giving all children the same opportunities to learn, play and take part in communities. For children with disability, it’s key to development.

Education rights for children with disability

Australian children with disability have the same rights to education as other children. These rights are protected by law. Read about your child’s rights.

Employment rights: teenagers with disability, autism or other additional needs

Young people with disability, autism or other needs have employment rights. They’re also entitled to some workplace adjustments so they can do the job well.

Being an advocate for children with disability, autism or other additional needs

Children with disability might need you to be an advocate for them. Advocating is speaking up for your child’s needs, interests and right to be included.
