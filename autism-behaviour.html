Autism: behaviour

Autism: understanding behaviour

Cooperative behaviour: autistic children and teenagers

You can help autistic children and teenagers with cooperation by setting limits, giving effective instructions, offering choices and changing the environment.

Building confidence: autistic children and teenagers

Build confidence in autistic children and teens by focusing on their strengths, interests and talents. Strategies like strengths cards and books can help.

Challenging behaviour: autistic children and teenagers

Challenging behaviour in autistic children and teens is common. When children learn to manage challenging behaviour, they can get along better with others.

Changing routines: autistic children and teenagers

Changing routines can be hard for autistic kids and teens. Preparing them for expected and unexpected change helps. Try social stories, schedules and more.

Obsessive behaviour, routines and rituals: autistic children and teenagers

Many autistic children and teens have routines, obsessions or rituals, which help them cope with stress. You can work on this behaviour if you need to.

Sensory sensitivities: autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children and teens sometimes have sensory sensitivities. They can be oversensitive or undersensitive to noise, light, clothes, temperature and so on.

Recognising, understanding and managing emotions: autistic children and teenagers

Autistic kids and teens might need support to recognise, understand and manage emotions. Everyday learning, emotions cards and thermometer ladders can help.

Autism: common behaviour concerns

Aggressive behaviour: autistic children and teenagers

Aggressive or self-injurious behaviour is fairly common in autistic children. The first step in managing aggressive behaviour is understanding why it happens.

Appointments: autistic children and teenagers

Appointments with dentists, doctors and hairdressers can be hard for autistic children and teenagers. Social stories, visual aids and preparation can help.

Meltdowns: autistic children and teenagers

Meltdowns happen when autistic kids and teens feel completely overwhelmed. To avoid or get through meltdowns, they need support to manage strong emotions.

Bullying: autistic children and teenagers

Bullying can be hard to spot in autistic children and teens, but there are physical, behaviour and emotional signs. You need to work with schools on bullying.

Stimming: autistic children and teenagers

Stimming is repetitive or unusual movements or noises. It seems to help some autistic children cope with stress. It might reduce as children develop skills.

Wandering: autistic children and teenagers

Many autistic children and teens wander. Depending on what causes the wandering, security gates, safety skills, ID bracelets and tracking apps can help.

Discipline strategies for autistic children and teenagers

Discipline strategies like praise, rules, consequences and social skills can help autistic children and teens behave well and avoid inappropriate behaviour.
