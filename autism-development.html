Autism:development

Autism: language development

Language development in autistic children

Autistic children can find it hard to learn and use language. You can help by creating reasons to use language, playing, modelling and rewarding language.

Augmentative and alternative communication: autistic children

Autistic children can have trouble with communication. An augmentative and alternative communication system might help your autistic child communicate.

Autism: physical development

Learning about bodies and personal boundaries: autistic children

Autistic children need to understand their bodies, private and public body parts, personal boundaries and safety. Visual supports and social stories can help.

Preparing for puberty: autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children entering puberty need support. Careful language, visual supports and social stories can help them understand how their bodies will change.

Autistic girls: periods

Start early with preparing autistic children for periods. It’s good to cover what periods are, period underwear, pads and tampons, and emotional symptoms.

Autism: sexual development

Sexuality and sexual development: autistic teenagers

Autistic teens can have trouble understanding sexual feelings and behaviour. Visual supports and social stories can support their healthy sexual development.

Romantic relationships and feelings: autistic teenagers

Visual supports, social stories and talking can help autistic teenagers recognise attraction and negotiate romantic relationships, respect and sensory issues.

Circle of friends: personal boundaries activity for children 3-15 years

A circle of friends activity shows different people in a child’s life. It helps children learn about personal boundaries and stay safe from sexual abuse.

Consent and sexual consent: autistic children and teenagers

Consent is essential to healthy relationships. If autistic children learn about consent early, you can introduce sexual consent as they move into the teens.

Problematic and harmful sexual behaviour in autistic children and teenagers

Autistic children and teenagers might need help to understand acceptable sexual behaviour. They need professional support for problematic sexual behaviour.

Autism: social & emotional development

Recognising, understanding and managing emotions: autistic children and teenagers

Autistic kids and teens might need support to recognise, understand and manage emotions. Everyday learning, emotions cards and thermometer ladders can help.

Meltdowns: autistic children and teenagers

Meltdowns happen when autistic kids and teens feel completely overwhelmed. To avoid or get through meltdowns, they need support to manage strong emotions.

Resilience in autistic children and teenagers

A calm environment, positive thinking, problem-solving, and emotional, social and organisational skills can help autistic children and teens with resilience.

Going out independently: autistic children and pre-teens

Learning to go out independently is good for children’s development. With support to build skills, many autistic children can learn to go out independently.

Going out independently: autistic teenagers

With the right supports many autistic teenagers can learn to go out independently. They can build skills and independence through gradual steps and practice.

Self-identity for autistic teenagers

Self-identity can be challenging for autistic teenagers. Try talking about diversity and personal strengths, finding peer groups and thinking about family.
