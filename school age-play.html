School age: play & development

Why play is important

Emotions and play: school-age children

Starting school can mean intense new emotions for kids. Play ideas to help children with emotions include drawing, painting, music, pretend play and reading.

Learning about emotions: activity for children 3-6 years

Learning about emotions helps children feel happier and cope better. This activity helps children practise have fun recognising and understanding emotions.

Imagining, creating and play: school-age children

Play fosters creativity in school kids, which is important for development. Play ideas include word games, pretend play, music, craft, nature walks and more.

Movement and play: school-age children

Play is key to motor skills development for school kids. Play ideas to get children moving include family activities, first sports, and walking to school.

Talking and play: school-age children

School children usually communicate well, although they still make mistakes. Boost language skills by reading, telling jokes, and playing simple word games.

Thinking and play: school-age children

School children develop thinking and learning skills through play. Play ideas for cognitive development include reading, puzzles, crosswords and board games.

Puppet play and emotions: activity for children 3-6 years

Puppet play is a fun way for children to learn about emotions. Use any puppet or soft toy or make your own. Follow your child’s lead with puppet play.

School age: getting play started

School-age children at play

Play is central to working out how to get along with other children and fit in. Here are some play ideas and games for kids in the early school years.

Letting your child lead play: activities for children 0-6 years

Child-led play is following your child’s lead and responding to what they say or do. It’s good because your child learns most when they’re engaged in play.

Toys and games for children

Toys and games for kids – what’s best? Finding the right toys and games is about matching play ideas to your child’s age, developmental stage and interests.

Choosing toys for children

The best toys for kids spark imagination, creativity and problem-solving skills. Open-ended toys are good for this. You can often make these toys at home.

School age: low-cost play ideas

Homemade toys and free activities for children

A little imagination can turn ordinary items into homemade toys and free activities for kids. Get ideas for babies, toddlers, preschoolers and school kids.

Stacking and building games: children 3-6 years

Stacking and building games are fun and good for your child’s creative learning, problem-solving and numeracy skills. Use blocks, boxes, cushions and so on.

Playdough activities: children 3-6 years

Playdough is a fun sensory experience for kids. It’s good for creativity, muscle strength and fine motor skills too. Our guide has homemade playdough recipes.

Cubbyhouse activity: children 2-6 years

Cubbyhouses are great for imaginative play, creative thinking and problem-solving. You can make a cubbyhouse with things around your home. Here are ideas.

Guess the animal game: activity for children 1-6 years

Our guess the animal game gets children using imagination and thinking skills. It can be good exercise too. You and your child can play this game anywhere.

Dress-up games: activity for children 3-6 years

Dress-up games let your child act out different roles, explore ideas, develop imagination, and express emotions in a safe way. Here’s how to get started.

Family story book activity: children 3-6 years

A family story book fosters your child’s sense of belonging and security. And making a story book with you can help to develop your child’s literacy skills.

Memory game for kids: 3-6 years

Memory games for kids build thinking skills, concentration and persistence. For this homemade memory game, you need some small objects, a tray and a cloth.

‘I spy’: turn-taking and talking activity for children 3-6 years

You and your children can play the ‘I spy’ game anywhere. It involves turn-taking and talking, so it’s good for your children’s social and language skills.

Collage: activity for children 2-6 years

Collage is a simple craft activity. Children paste materials onto paper. Collage is great for fine motor skills and colour and texture awareness.

Playing with cardboard boxes: activities for children 2-6 years

Cardboard boxes are great for imaginative play. Your child can use them to make castles, shops, caves and more. Cardboard boxes also encourage physical play.

School age: outdoor play

Outdoor play

Outdoor play is a big part of growth, development and wellbeing for children. Outdoor play ideas include tummy time, ball play, nature walks and bike rides.

Noticing nature walk: activity for children 3-6 years

A noticing nature walk encourages your child to observe with all senses. All you need is somewhere to walk. Questions can prompt your child to notice things.

Listening walk: activity for children 4-6 years

On a listening walk, your child tries to focus on sounds. A listening walk helps young children learn to focus. It can also be an early mindfulness activity.

School age: active play

Rough-and-tumble play: a guide

Rough play and play fighting can be fun. It can also help children develop social and physical skills. Ground rules can prevent children from getting hurt.

Physical activity for school-age children

Trying different sports helps school-age children work out what they’re good at. Doing physical activity they enjoy and are good at keeps them interested.

Sport: encouraging children to have a positive attitude

Kids and sport – it’s a great mix. You can encourage your child to be a good sport by role-modelling a positive attitude and praising your child’s efforts.

Playing with balls: activities for children 3-6 years

Playing with balls is fun. It’s also good for muscle development, hand-eye coordination and fitness. You can make your own balls with rolled socks or paper.

Treasure hunt: activity for children 3-6 years

An outdoor treasure hunt game encourages children to be active and to explore. You set up a treasure hunt by making and hiding clues as well as the treasure.

Rough-and-tumble play: activities for children 3 months-8 years

Rough-and-tumble play is climbing, wrestling, rolling around and play fighting. It’s fun and good exercise. Get ideas for rough-and-tumble play activities.

School age: playing with others

Sharing and learning to share

Learning to share can be a challenge for children, but it’s key to getting along with others. Practice, encouragement and sharing games can help kids learn.

Fair play for children

In children’s games, playing fair is more important than winning. Teach your child about fair play by being a role model and choosing appropriate games.

School age: media influences, benefits & choices

How children see TV, YouTube, games and movies

When kids watch TV, YouTube, games and movies, violence, sex and scary images affect them in various ways. Think about your child’s age when choosing media.

Good apps, games, TV shows, movies and YouTube for school-age children

If good-quality apps, games, YouTube, movies and TV have positive messages and spark thinking, they can encourage good behaviour and help school kids learn.

Advertising: how it influences children and teenagers

The ability to spot advertising and understand how adverts work is an important life skill for kids and teens. You can help your child develop this skill.

Disaster news and distressing news events: supporting children 6-11 years

Children hear and see media coverage of natural disasters, terrorism, violence, accidents and the pandemic. Talking is the best way to help your child cope.

School age: screen time & healthy screen use

Screen time: checklist for healthy use

Screen time can be part of a healthy lifestyle for your children. Just balance screen time with other activities and encourage kids to use quality content.

School-age screen time: tips for balance

Screen time can be part of a healthy lifestyle for school-age kids when balanced with physical play, creative play, reading, socialising and other activities.

Managing screen time: strategies for children 3-11 years

Healthy screen time habits for kids don’t always come easy. Strategies for managing screen time can help. You can try rules, routines, transitions and choices.

Shared screen time: why it’s good for you and your child

Sharing screen time with your child is fun, builds your relationship and helps you both learn new things. It turns screen time into family time. Get tips.

Sharing screen time with children: tips

Sharing screen time means choosing age-appropriate content, watching or playing with your child, and talking together about games, shows or videos. Get tips.

Screen time: how it helps children learn

Screen time can help children learn when children use good-quality digital media. It can help children develop problem-solving, social and creative skills.

Screen time and physical problems: reducing the risks

Too much screen time, especially with bad posture, can cause physical problems like eye strain, neck pain and repetitive strain. Here’s how to reduce risk.

Healthy posture for children’s screen time: in pictures

When children are enjoying screen time, healthy posture helps them avoid pain, strain and discomfort. Encourage children to change position and move around.

Using screen time to encourage physical activity: 3-11 years

You can use screen time to get more physical activity into your child’s day. Tips include digital walking maps, sports apps, physical skills videos and more.

Screen time and social life: children and teenagers

Screen time and screen use can be good for your child’s friendships, family relationships and social life. It can also help your child develop social skills.

Screen time and sleep: children and teenagers

Did you know that screen time can affect your child’s sleep? You can reduce risks by encouraging your child to avoid digital media in the hour before bed.

Video games, online games and apps: children and teenagers

Video games, online games and apps are very popular with children and teens. If you help kids make good choices, they can get benefits and avoid problems.

Healthy video gaming for children and teenagers

Video gaming for children can be fun and worthwhile when your child has healthy gaming habits like self-regulation, balance, and safety and respect online.

School age: online safety

Internet safety: children 6-8 years

Children can come across dangerous things online. Internet safety includes going online with children, role-modelling, and talking about online behaviour.

Sexting: early conversations with children 6-11 years

Sexting is using digital technology to send sexual images, videos or messages. Talk with kids about sexting, sharing images and behaving respectfully online.

Pornography: talking about it with children 5-8 years

Talking about pornography doesn’t rob children of innocence. It’s one of the best ways to protect children at 5-8 years. Internet safety guidelines help too.

Grooming: recognising the signs

Grooming is when a person gets friendly with a child or family for later sexual abuse. Signs include a person taking special interest in a family or child.
