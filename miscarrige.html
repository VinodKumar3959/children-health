Pregnancy: miscarriage & stillbirth

Miscarriage

Miscarriage: what it is and how to cope

Miscarriage is when a fetus dies before 20 weeks. It happens in about one in five pregnancies. Common miscarriage signs are vaginal bleeding and cramps.

Stillbirth and neonatal death

Stillbirth: a guide

Stillbirth is physically demanding and distressing. After stillbirth, you’ll be able to spend time with your baby, making memories in your own way.

Neonatal death: a guide

Neonatal death is when a baby dies within the first 28 days of life. It’s very distressing. Spending time with your baby after death can help with grief.

Grief after stillbirth or neonatal death

When a baby dies because of stillbirth or neonatal death, there’s no right way for you to grieve. People deal with grief in their own ways.

Stillbirth: reducing the risks

You can reduce risk of stillbirth by eating well and exercising, sleeping on your side, and seeking immediate medical help if your baby’s movements change.
