Dad's Guide to pregnancy

Dads & early pregnancy



Men: your reactions to pregnancy

Whether a pregnancy is planned or a surprise, hearing that you’re going to be a father is huge. This Dads Guide covers men’s feelings about early pregnancy.

Pregnancy: where do men fit in?

Some men feel left out in pregnancy. Some even feel jealous of pregnancy. If you need help with feelings, talk to your partner, or a friend or counsellor.

Men's feelings in early pregnancy: not into it?

In early pregnancy, it’s normal for men to feel uninterested or just ‘not into it’. Read more about men’s feelings during pregnancy in our Dads Guide.

Thinking about being a dad in pregnancy

‘What does being a dad mean to me?’ Thinking about this in pregnancy can set men up for a rewarding experience as a father. Our Dads Guide explains.

Sex in early pregnancy: men

Sex in pregnancy is usually fine, but many men find their sexual relationship changes, especially in early pregnancy. Read more in our Dads Guide.

Moods and morning sickness: a guide for men

Most women have mood changes and morning sickness in pregnancy. Our Dads Guide explains how men can help their partners cope with this part of pregnancy.

Scans and screening in the first trimester: for dads

This Dads Guide to Pregnancy article covers early pregnancy scans and screening, plus normal fetal development and early miscarriage – for men, by men.

Miscarriage: a guide for men

This Dads Guide to Pregnancy covers miscarriage, the grief men might experience after miscarriage, and how to support partners after pregnancy loss.

Exercise and your partner in early pregnancy

How much exercise is safe for your partner in early pregnancy? Unless a doctor says otherwise, light to moderate exercise is OK. This Dads Guide explains.

Smoking, alcohol and other drugs in pregnancy: men

If smoking, drinking alcohol or taking drugs is part of your lifestyle, your partner’s pregnancy might be the time to quit. Get information for dads-to-be.

Dads: money, budgets and providing for your family

Many dads and dads-to-be worry about being a family provider. Our Dads Guide takes you through options for money and budgets in pregnancy.

Dads and work-life balance: putting work into perspective

For many dads, it can take time to find a work-life balance. But having a family can put work challenges into perspective, as our Dads Guide explains.

Dads: preparing for relationship changes after baby

A baby’s arrival can put extra pressure on men’s relationships with partners. Our Dads Guide to Pregnancy can help you prepare for relationship changes.

Men: anger and violence in pregnancy

Many men feel stressed in pregnancy. For some, pregnancy can trigger anger and violence. If anger in pregnancy is an issue for you, our Dads Guide can help.

Dads & middle pregnancy

What men can expect in middle pregnancy

Tiredness and morning sickness often ease up in pregnancy’s second trimester. Our guide explains how this can be a special time for men and their partners.

Men’s feelings in middle pregnancy: not real yet?

It’s pretty common for men to feel pregnancy is happening at a distance. Even in the second trimester, it might not feel real. Read more in our Dads Guide.

Sex in middle pregnancy: men

In the second trimester, women might go through body and hormone changes that affect their desire for sex. Our Dads Guide describes how this can affect men.

Stress during pregnancy: for men

Pregnancy can be stressful for men. Staying relaxed is better for your health – and baby’s. Our Dads Guide has tips to help men handle stress in pregnancy.

The 20-week ultrasound scan: for dads

The 20-week scan checks that your baby is developing as expected. You can find out the sex of your baby if you want. This Dads Guide is for men, by men.

Exercise and your partner in the second trimester

How much exercise is safe for pregnant women in the second trimester? Light to moderate exercise is usually OK. This Dads Guide explains and has tips.

Building your relationship in pregnancy: dads

The middle months of pregnancy can be a great time for men to strengthen relationships with their partners. This Dads Guide to Pregnancy explains how.

Birth classes: what they are and why they’re good

Birth classes help you and your partner get ready for labour, birth, breastfeeding and early parenting. Our Dads Guide explains why they’re good for men.

Birth classes just for men

In birth or antenatal classes for men, you learn about supporting your partner in birth and becoming a dad. You can get support from other dads-to-be.

Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander dads

Our Dads Guide has tips for Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander men on being a dad and supporting partners during birth, breastfeeding and early parenting.

Benefits of work-life balance for dads

A work-life balance has many benefits for men, their partners and their families. Our Dads Guide explains why balance is important and how to achieve it.

Work-life balance stories: dads

Work-life balance often isn’t easy – but it’s worth it. In our Dads Guide, fathers share their stories of juggling work, a new baby and family life.

Dads & late pregnancy

What men can expect in late pregnancy

The third trimester is an exciting time, as baby’s birth gets closer. It’s a time for men to prepare for a birth support role and their first hours as dads.

Dad and Partner Pay

If your partner has had a baby or you’ve adopted a child, you might be able to get Dad and Partner Pay, an entitlement under the Paid Parental Leave Scheme.

Dads: including you in antenatal care

Health professionals are getting better at involving men in antenatal care. As an expectant dad, you might need to ask questions and show interest. Read tips.

Men’s feelings in late pregnancy: still not real?

For many men, the third trimester is when pregnancy starts to feel real. But if you’re still getting your head around it, our Dads Guide has tips to help.

Sex in late pregnancy: men

It’s usually fine to have sex in pregnancy, but in the third trimester it might feel different or awkward. Our Dads Guide describes how this can affect men.

Antenatal and postnatal depression: fathers and all non-birthing parents

Fathers and all non-birthing parents can get antenatal and postnatal depression. Seek help for emotional and other changes that last longer than 2 weeks.

Pregnancy complications in the third trimester: for dads

Pregnancy complications like pre-eclampsia or unusual baby development can make the third trimester an anxious time. Our Dads Guide has information for men.

Men: planning your family support network

Late pregnancy is a time when men can plan a family support network to help them and their families after baby arrives. Our Dads Guide has tips and ideas.

Nesting in late pregnancy: a guide for men

As a dad-to-be, you might have noticed your partner getting stuff ready for baby. This is nesting and it’s normal in pregnancy, as our Dads Guide explains.

New dads: time with your partner, time for yourself

For new dads, making time for yourself and your partner can be challenging. This Dads Guide has tips to help you plan this time and make the most of it.
